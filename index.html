♻ Waste & materials

biodegradable

/ˌbaɪ.əʊ.dɪˈɡreɪ.də.bəl/

có thể phân hủy sinh học

Able to break down naturally without harming the environment.

Example: The café uses biodegradable food containers.

◉ Climate & carbon

carbon footprint

/ˌkɑː.bən ˈfʊt.prɪnt/

dấu chân carbon; lượng khí thải carbon

The total carbon emissions caused by a person, product, or activity.

Example: Taking the bus can reduce your carbon footprint.

◎ Climate & carbon

carbon neutral

/ˌkɑː.bən ˈnjuː.trəl/

trung hòa carbon

Producing no net carbon emissions overall.

Example: The company aims to become carbon neutral by 2035.

⌁ Climate & carbon

climate resilience

/ˈklaɪ.mət rɪˈzɪl.i.əns/

khả năng chống chịu với biến đổi khí hậu

The ability to prepare for and recover from climate-related problems.

Example: Flood barriers can improve a city's climate resilience.

⚠ Pollution

contamination

/kənˌtæm.ɪˈneɪ.ʃən/

sự ô nhiễm; sự nhiễm bẩn

The process of making something dirty, polluted, or unsafe.

Example: Chemical contamination made the lake unsafe.

▽ Resources

depletion

/dɪˈpliː.ʃən/

sự cạn kiệt

The reduction or exhaustion of a resource.

Example: Overfishing can lead to resource depletion.

✓ Responsible choices

eco-conscious

/ˌiː.kəʊˈkɒn.ʃəs/

có ý thức bảo vệ môi trường

Aware of environmental problems and making greener choices.

Example: Eco-conscious shoppers avoid unnecessary plastic.

◌ Nature

ecosystem

/ˈiː.kəʊˌsɪs.təm/

hệ sinh thái

A community of living things and their natural environment.

Example: Oil spills can damage a marine ecosystem.

↓ Environmental change

environmental degradation

/ɪnˌvaɪ.rənˈmen.təl ˌdeɡ.rəˈdeɪ.ʃən/

sự suy thoái môi trường

The worsening or destruction of the natural environment.

Example: Deforestation causes environmental degradation.

↗ Environmental change

environmental impact

/ɪnˌvaɪ.rənˈmen.təl ˈɪm.pækt/

tác động môi trường

The effect that an action or activity has on the environment.

Example: Factories should study their environmental impact.

♙ People

environmentalist

/ɪnˌvaɪ.rənˈmen.təl.ɪst/

nhà hoạt động môi trường

A person who works to protect the natural environment.

Example: The environmentalist campaigns to protect forests.

☁ Climate & carbon

greenhouse gas

/ˈɡriːn.haʊs ɡæs/

khí nhà kính

A gas that traps heat in the atmosphere.

Example: Carbon dioxide is a major greenhouse gas.

▤ Waste & materials

landfill

/ˈlænd.fɪl/

bãi chôn lấp rác

A place where waste is buried in the ground.

Example: Plastic may remain in a landfill for hundreds of years.

✧ Pollution

pollutant

/pəˈluː.tənt/

chất gây ô nhiễm

A substance that pollutes air, water, or soil.

Example: The authorities must identify the main pollutant.

≈ Resources

reservoir

/ˈrez.ə.vwɑːr/

hồ chứa nước

A natural or artificial lake used to store water.

Example: The city stores drinking water in a reservoir.

∞ Responsible choices

sustainability

/səˌsteɪ.nəˈbɪl.ə.ti/

tính bền vững

The ability to meet present needs without harming future generations.

Example: Renewable energy supports long-term sustainability.

! Pollution

toxic

/ˈtɒk.sɪk/

độc hại

Poisonous or harmful to people, animals, or the environment.

Example: The factory released toxic chemicals into the river.

▥ Environmental change

urbanization

/ˌɜː.bən.aɪˈzeɪ.ʃən/

đô thị hóa

The growth of cities and the movement of people into urban areas.

Example: Rapid urbanization can reduce green space.

◇ Nature

wildlife

/ˈwaɪld.laɪf/

động vật hoang dã

Animals and plants living naturally in an area.

Example: The wetland provides a home for local wildlife.
